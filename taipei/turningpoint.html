review

There are a lot of Japanese bars in Taipei. You probably heard of Tomoaki (WaShu part of this list), or maybe the amazing Yoshi (Bar Between). But have you heard of Masahiro Kon? Many bartenders speak highly of him. That got me curious. And indeed. Turning Point is a very simple, small, modest and professional Japanese bar. Don’t expect fussy colorful cocktail, or expensive fancy bottles.

I ordered the most simple cocktail. It hard to make a 2 ingredients cocktail feel "special". But Masahiro somehow managed to. I don’t want to go too much into the details of fear of blowing up my cover, but the thinness of the glass or the carbonation level made that drink feel so sharp, highlighting the taste of the liquor in the best way.

So you go to Turning Point for the drinks. Expect quality, precision, real flavors, and professionalism.

I would usually go to Turning Point by myself. The great Jazz, that large bar, the dimmed light, the sobriety of the place and the company of Masahiro, with a delicious drink, this a good way to end the day.

Good for: The Jazz, the large bar, the sobriety of the place, and the Japanese professionalism.

recommendation for dinner:

There’s a nice Soba noodles restaurant near by called (二月半そば)[https://www.facebook.com/healthy.soba]. We like the XO cold noodles. We also recommend (BeApe)[https://www.facebook.com/beape.french/], a french restaurant. Get some french fries and mousles for a starter. We also recommend the lamb.
