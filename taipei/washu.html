review

Located in Dunhua.

The owner, and also the "master" of the place, is named Tomoaki Inaba. He’s Japanese. The place looks like a chemistry lab. It’s a small place (I will provide pictures for you get a sense of what it looks like).

The main thing in this bar is that the cocktails are very much unique. The drinks are genius. For example, Tomoaki would mix peanut butter and whisky, or maybe banana. Smoked chocolate? Wazabi? Pomelo and chili? The rule appears to be that if you can eat it, you can drink it.

During the night, while the bartender mix you a drink, you can witness Tomoaki experimenting with new recipes.

Recommendation: in the menu, skip the fruit cocktails and go straight to the spices and herbs cocktail. Before leaving the place, ask for a shiso or thyme Martini.

Tomoaki sent me this:

經過了在東京、倫敦、蘇格蘭的酒吧、餐廳、釀造所、蒸餾所的修行之後，偶然地愛上了台北這個不夜城，「WA-SHU」日式酒吧就這樣誕生了。 「WA-SHU」背負著傳達日本酒文化的使命，本店是宣傳、教育型的飲食店，集結了各式各樣日本本地生產的酒類。

本店不定時舉辦各種研討會、學習會，以能積極地推銷新商品、提供宣傳活動、宣傳資料等等作為目標，更希望藉由本店的存在，進而使台灣觀光客對日本旅遊、日本文化有更深入的了解與興趣。 另外，對日本觀光客也免費提供台灣觀光或周邊區域旅遊美食等訊息，店內有個小區域擺放了其他各種類店舖的名片、簡介等。
